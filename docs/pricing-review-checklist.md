# Pricing Analysis Review Checklist

Before relying on a v1.7.0 pricing publication, confirm:

1. Demand curves are supported by recent, representative evidence.
2. Observed points cover the decision-relevant price range; endpoint clamping is not mistaken for extrapolation.
3. Segment multipliers do not double-count customers.
4. Capacity reflects the same time period as demand.
5. Unit variable, fulfillment, channel, and fixed costs are complete.
6. The selected objective—revenue, contribution, or profit—matches the decision.
7. Break-even quantity is reviewed alongside capacity and minimum-volume constraints.
8. Price-change limits, legal requirements, accessibility, equity, contracts, taxes, and competitor responses are reviewed outside the model.
9. The source workspace, scenario, and revision identifiers match the approved evidence record.
